Social Networks

Social networks are computer-hosted websites or services that allow humans to connect and share information about themselves with another human virtually.

The first social network, SixDegrees, was founded by Andrew Weinreich in 1997.
Named after the six degrees of seperation theory, the website uses a social-circle model, one of the first social networks to pioneer the form. Users were allowed to send messages between people in their 1st, 2nd, and 3rd degrees, but not beyond that. They could also see their connection to other users.

This makes SixDegrees the first social media service to pioneer this form, leaving room for other websites like Facebook to continue innovating prior to the creation of SixDegrees.

Concepts of the Social-Circle Model

Entity

An entity in the context of social networks is a person who has a presence. They are often identified through their username, profile pic or activities they do.
An example of an entity would be a creator of a Facebook group.

Relationship

Relationships are connections between 2 or more entities.

They can be formed from common interests or mutual friends and can be categorised into 3 categories: friends, family, and acquaintances.

Sociogram
