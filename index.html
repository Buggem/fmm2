About this website

This website is an informative source of networking knowledge.

We have several articles to inform you on various topics related to computer networks, which are collections of computers linked to eachother directly or indirectly. See the page on network topology for more info.

Basic terminology about Computer Networks

ARPANET

ARPANET (Advanced Research Projects Agency Network) refers to a large military computer network, lasting 21 years from 1969, acting as a prototype, laying foundations for what would become the internet.
It was decommissioned in 1990 following the rise of the internet.

Bandwidth

Network bandwidth is a measurement indicating the maximum capacity of a wired or wireless communications link to transmit data over a network connection in a given amount of time.

Binary

Binary is a format of storing data, consisting of only 1’s and 0’s.
This numeric system, also known as base-2, is the current most used standard for storing information on computer systems.

Decimal

A decimal is a mathematical way of representing non-whole numbers. Computers can convert binary into human-readable base-10 decimals in accordance with specifications such as the IEEE 754 floating-point standard.

Bits, Bytes, KiloBytes and Megabytes

Bits, Bytes, Kilobytes, Megabytes, Gigabytes, and so on are ways of measuring the size of computer data.
A bit is a single binary numeral - either a 1 or a 0. A byte is 8 bits.
Each variation of a bit or byte multiplies the previous various by 1000.

In accordance to this:

  • A byte is 8 bits
  • A kilobit is 1,000 bits
  • A kilobyte is 8,000 bits
  • A megabit is 1,000,000 bits
  • A megabyte is 8,000,000 bits
  • A gigabit is 1,000,000 bits
  • A gigabyte is 8,000,000,000 bits and so on.

Cloud-based data storage

A mode of computer data storage in which digital data is stored on servers in off-site locations. The servers are maintained by a third-party provider who is responsible for hosting, managing and securing data stored on its infrastructure.

Data

Data refers to any statistic that can be contained within a system, for example graphs on paper.

In the case of computers, data is often stored in the binary format and sent across the internet.

Information

Information is a general term that refers to any data that can inform us or our devices of an event or statistic.

Internet of Me (IoMe)

The Internet of Me (IoMe) loosely refers to technology which connects our minds and bodies with the online world. It transforms our biological and cognitive life into streams of data which can be monitored, shared and shaped.

Internet of Things (IoT)

Internet of Things (IoT) encompasses smart devices and smart objects that send and receive information using the internet and communication infrastructure.
